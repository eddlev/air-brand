01 At a glance

A framework for structured AI work.

AIR is a prompt-based framework — a governing layer that configures the environment, organizes the work, then executes. It runs on whatever model you bring.

Lead with framework in public language. Describe what it does as a governing layer; describe how it works in practice as a runtime. Never assume the reader already knows the word "runtime."

The honest disclaimer · use once, where it matters

AIR is a prompt-based framework. It runs on the model you bring, so results depend on that model and can vary. AIR adds structure and surfaces uncertainty — it doesn't guarantee correctness. Keep your judgment in the loop.

02 Positioning & strategy

A structured generalist, not a vertical tool.

AIR is a domain-agnostic structure layer for any AI work — development, research, writing, brand, world-building. It competes on discipline and honesty, not on a niche feature set.

The four pillars

01

Configure, then execute

Set up the environment before doing the work. Map first, act second.

02

Honest by design

Prompt-compiled, not backend-validated. It surfaces uncertainty instead of hiding it.

03

Benchmark before delivery

Hold the work to a task-scoped standard before it ships.

04

Yours anywhere

Portable and sovereign. No single-provider lock-in.

The promise

Structure before output. Evidence before claims. Honesty before hype.

05 Logo system

The dot-in-frame mark.

A brass rounded-square frame holds an ember dot. The frame is the framework; the dot is the active step. The set includes primary, stacked, and wordmark lockups, plus mono and favicon forms.

Mark

Pure geometry. Works at any size.

AIR

Primary

Mark + wordmark lockup.

Rules

  • Clear space: keep at least the height of the dot clear on every side.
  • Minimum size: the mark stays legible to 16px (use the favicon form below that).
  • Don't recolor, stretch, rotate, add effects, or fill the frame.
  • Ember is reserved for the dot — never restyle it as a generic accent here.

08 Graphic language

Frames and the active dot.

The dot-in-frame is a grammar, not just a logo. Brass frames contain content (the framework holding the work); the ember dot marks what's live. Banners and templates use the frame as a container; ember stays rare.

  • Use the brass frame to enclose lockups, cards, and section anchors.
  • Reserve the ember dot for the single active or focal element.
  • Quiet, structural watermarks of the frame are allowed; keep them low-contrast.

09 Web & UI

Building the interface.

The website and any UI follow the same system: a warm-dark foundation, generous whitespace, the brass frame for structure, and ember reserved for the single live element on screen.

Components

Primary actions use ember on dark ink; secondary use a brass outline; tertiary stay quiet. Cards sit on --surface with a hairline border and the large radius.

Layout & spacing

  • Center content in a readable column; let whitespace carry the calm, precise tone.
  • Use the brass frame to enclose hero lockups, cards, and section anchors.
  • Step spacing on the token scale: 4 · 8 · 12 · 16 · 24 · 32 · 48 · 64.

Motion & accessibility

  • Motion is subtle and purposeful — short eases (.2–.4s), no decorative animation.
  • Respect prefers-reduced-motion; keep a visible brass focus ring; ensure full keyboard navigation.
  • Text on foundation meets AA contrast; never rely on ember alone to convey meaning.

14 Governance & honesty

The principles underneath.

AIR's credibility rests on not overclaiming. These hold across every surface:

  • Prompt-compiled origin — the framework is built from prompts, not a validated backend.
  • backend_validation_claimed: false — stated plainly, never hidden.
  • Fail-closed — when something can't be supported, it stops rather than guesses.
  • Keep human judgment in the loop — AIR structures and surfaces; it doesn't certify.

Using the AIR marks

The framework code is Apache-2.0. The brand — the AIR and VM4AI names and the dot-in-frame mark — is reserved.

Welcome
  • Add the "Made with AIR" stamp to genuine AIR-built projects, linked to vm4ai.com.
  • Refer to AIR by name in articles, talks, and reviews.
  • Use the unmodified logo when crediting or linking to AIR.
Not allowed
  • Implying endorsement, affiliation, or official partnership.
  • Altering the mark's color, proportions, or the ember dot.
  • Using the marks in your own product name, logo, or domain.

16 Do / don't

Quick rules.

Do
  • Lead with "framework"; explain "runtime."
  • Use brass freely for structure; ember rarely.
  • Keep the disclaimer to one decisive placement.
  • Self-host the fonts for full sovereignty.
Don't
  • Lead with the expansion — AIR is the wordmark; state “AI Resource” once, then use AIR.
  • Recolor or fill the mark, or restyle ember as decoration.
  • Claim guaranteed or validated correctness.
  • Use teal or the generic purple-blue AI gradient.
